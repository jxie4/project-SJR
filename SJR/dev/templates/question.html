What is the relationship between political system status and social development?

We are interested in understanding how the political system status interacts with the social development indicators in different countries. We selected a wide range of indicators to see how they correlate with political system status.

Indicators

Indicators used in our studies mainly come from two datasources, World Development Indicator and Polity IV. For more information, click on datasource in the navigation bar.

For political system status , we use an indicator polity2 taken from the project Polity IV. This indicator ranges from +10 (strongly democratic) to !10 (strongly autocratic). The indicator is calculated using a scoring mechanism that measures institutionalized democracy and institutionalized autocracy. They each take into consider different perspectives of democracy and autocracy and come up with a score on a scale from 0-10. By subtracting the 2 indicators, we will be able to get polity2.

On the other hand, to see how social development changes over time, we've selected 10 indicators from WDI in 3 different dimensions: education, employment and health. Here's a full list of indicators used in our analysis.

  • Education

    1. School enrollment (secondary) by gender

  • Employment (ILO Model)

    1. Employment in agriculture (%)
    2. Employment in services (%)
    3. Labor force participation rate, age 15-24, by gender
    4. Unemployment, age 15-24, by gender

  • Health

    1. Mortality rate under 5
    2. Mortality rate, adult by gender
    3. Life expectancy at birth by gender
    4. Survival to age 65 by gender
    5. Fertility rate by gender
