What is the relationship between political system status and social development?

We are interested in understanding how the political system status interacts with the social development indicators in different countries. We selected a wide range of indicators to see how they correlate with political system status.

Indicators

Indicators used in our studies mainly come from two data sources, World Development Indicators (WDI) and the Polity IV Project. For more information, click on Dataset Source under About in the navigation bar.

For political system status , we use an indicator polity2 taken from the Polity IV Project. This indicator ranges from +10 (strongly democratic) to -10 (strongly autocratic). The indicator is calculated using a scoring mechanism that measures institutionalized democracy and institutionalized autocracy. They each take into account different perspectives of democracy and autocracy and come up with a score on a scale from 0-10. By subtracting the 2 indicators, we are able to get polity2.

On the other hand, to see how social development changes over time, we've selected 10 indicators from WDI in 3 different areas: education, employment and health. Here's a full list of indicators used in our analysis.

  • Education

    1. School enrollment (secondary) by gender

  • Employment

    1. Employment in agriculture (%)
    2. Employment in services (%)
    3. Labor force participation rate for age 15-24 (%) (ILO Model)
    4. Unemployment for age 15-24 (%) (ILO Model)

  • Health

    1. Mortality rate under 5
    2. Mortality rate, adult by gender
    3. Life expectancy at birth
    4. Fertility rate
In our Key Findings section, we've presented the data for each country in the tab Country Overview. The Indicator Overview tab, you can select a number of countries and one single indicator. The graph will present the results comparing one indicator over multiple countries.
